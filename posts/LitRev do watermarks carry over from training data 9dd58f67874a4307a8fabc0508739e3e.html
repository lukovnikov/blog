LitRev: do watermarks carry over from training data into generated samples?

Artificial Fingerprinting for Generative Models: Rooting Deepfake Attribution in Training Data

by Yu et al., ICCV 2021

Citations

Motivation:

Introduce artificial fingerprints into models such that we can more easily detect and attribute deepfakes.

Approach overview:

Integrate into the training images an artificial fingerprint/watermark that enables identification and tracing. When a generative model is trained on such a watermarked training dataset, the images sampled from the generator also contain the same watermark.

Contributions:

  • deep image steganography for deepfake detection and attribution
  • first study to demonstrate the fingerprints from training data carry over to images sampled from a generator trained on those data (transferability)
  • empirically validate that it holds for different generators, does not severely affect generation quality, is robust to various perturbations and is hard to detect.

Problem Statement:

Fingerprinting is done from the perspective of model creator in order to later be able to verify that a generated images is generated using their model that they released. The fingerprint encoder, decoder, and the fingerprints are kept private.

Approach:

Image attribution aims to identify what generator an image came from or if it is real. Under a closed-world assumption, where we assume to know all possible generators, this problem can be posed as a multi-class classification problem. However, in reality, we can not know all possible generators (open-world assumption).

In this paper’s approach, the identity of the generator is represented by an artifical fingerprint w∈{0,1}nw \in \{0, 1\}^nw∈{0,1}n﻿. Their approach consists of learning an encoder EE E﻿ that embeds such fingerprints into images and a decoder DDD﻿ that takes an image and decodes a fingerprint. They are trained independently of any generator by minimizing a combination of an image reconstruction loss and a watermark reconstruction loss:

With EEE﻿ and DDD﻿ trained, we can add a specified fingerprint to the entire training set of images XXX﻿ and use it for training a generative model GGG﻿. It is hypothesized that the fingerprints from the images are transfered to the images generated by GGG﻿. The fingerprint decoder DDD﻿ can be applied on the generated images in order to recover the fingerprints.

The essential property of the approach is the transfer of fingerprints from the training dataset to the generated images. This and other properties of the fingerprinting approach are tested.
